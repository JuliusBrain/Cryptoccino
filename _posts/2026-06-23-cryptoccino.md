## Taiko Shuts Down Its L2 After Bridge Exploit Drains $1.7M and Token Slides.
{: #taiko-shuts-down-its-l2-after-bridge-exploit-drains-17m-and-token-slides}

[`coindesk`](https://www.coindesk.com/tech/2026/06/22/taiko-halts-its-ethereum-layer-2-network-after-a-bridge-exploit-token-dives-10) [`cointelegraph_defi`](https://cointelegraph.com/news/taiko-urges-users-to-withdraw-as-bridge-exploit-drains-17m) [`decrypt`](https://decrypt.co/371769/ethereum-layer-2-taiko-withdraw-bridge-funds-security-breach)
{: .sources}

<div class="fng-chip" aria-label="Crypto Fear &amp; Greed Index" title="Crypto Fear &amp; Greed Index. Scale 0 (extreme fear) to 100 (extreme greed)."><span class="fng-label">Fear &amp; Greed</span><span class="fng-value">23<span class="fng-scale">/100</span></span><span class="fng-class">Extreme Fear</span> <span class="fng-delta flat">±0 / 7d</span></div>

**What happened.** Taiko's Ethereum L2 bridge and ERC20 Vault were compromised via a flaw in the chain state verification mechanism, allowing attackers to forge proofs and pull out roughly $1.7 million in unauthorised withdrawals. The team halted the network and issued an urgent advisory for users to withdraw remaining bridge funds while remediation is underway.

**Why it matters.** This lands in a quarter that is already shaping up as the worst on record for crypto security: Cointelegraph counts 83 incidents and $755 million stolen in Q2 2026 alone, with cross-chain bridges the costliest vector. Proof verification is supposed to be the security guarantee of ZK-adjacent systems, so a forged-proof exploit here will put scrutiny on verification design across the broader L2 landscape.

**The catch.** The Fear &amp; Greed Index is parked at 23 — extreme fear, unchanged for a week — so any secondary selling pressure from the TAIKO token slide hits a market with no cushion. Bridge exploits rarely stop at the first withdrawal before the team reacts, and the full loss figure may climb once the post-mortem is published.
