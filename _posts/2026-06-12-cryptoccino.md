## Bitcoin's debasement trade is unwinding, half the supply is in the red, and corporate buyers have gone quiet.
{: #bitcoins-debasement-trade-is-unwinding-half-the-supply-is-in-the-red-and-corporate-buyers-have-gone-quiet}

[`bloomberg_crypto`](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-06-10/bitcoin-selloff-leaves-half-of-all-supply-trading-at-a-loss) [`coindesk`](https://www.coindesk.com/markets/2026/06/11/it-s-not-just-bitcoin-etfs-corporate-btc-buying-has-dried-up-too) [`decrypt`](https://decrypt.co/370749/bitcoin-etfs-shed-2-1b-in-june-so-far-as-market-selloff-deepens) [`theblock`](https://www.theblock.co/post/404471/jpmorgan-debasement-trade-retreat-bitcoin-gold) [`coindesk`](https://www.coindesk.com/markets/2026/06/11/bitcoin-has-reached-a-deep-bear-market-valuation-zone-the-hard-part-may-come-next)
{: .sources}

<div class="fng-chip" aria-label="Crypto Fear &amp; Greed Index" title="Crypto Fear &amp; Greed Index. Scale 0 (extreme fear) to 100 (extreme greed)."><span class="fng-label">Fear &amp; Greed</span><span class="fng-value">12<span class="fng-scale">/100</span></span><span class="fng-class">Extreme Fear</span> <span class="fng-delta flat">±0 / 7d</span></div>

**What happened.** More than half of all circulating bitcoin supply is now held at an unrealised loss after a multi-week selloff pushed BTC to the $63,000 range. Spot ETFs have shed roughly $2.1 billion in June alone, and corporate treasury buying has collapsed from a peak near $500 million per day to nearly nothing. JPMorgan analysts say the retreat from the debasement trade has accelerated for bitcoin even as gold holds firmer.

**Why it matters.** The Crypto Fear and Greed Index is sitting at 12, deep in extreme fear territory, with zero net recovery over the past seven days. That combination of on-chain loss dominance, ETF outflows, and a vanishing corporate bid suggests the reflexive institutional buyer of 2024 has stepped back, leaving a thinner demand stack. On-chain valuation models are now signalling levels historically associated with bear-market capitulation floors, but those floors have a habit of breaking before they hold.

**The catch.** ETF outflows may be less catastrophic than they look: one CoinDesk analysis argues recent redemptions are partly arbitrage unwinds rather than pure directional selling, implying basis traders exiting rather than long-only holders fleeing. Still, with corporate buyers absent and sentiment pinned, any near-term recovery depends on a macro catalyst that is not yet visible. Jack Mallers is calling this a global liquidity crisis; Michael Saylor and he are now openly sparring over which bitcoin reporting metrics best capture the damage.
