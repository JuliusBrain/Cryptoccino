## Israel-Iran Strikes Knock Bitcoin Off Its Recovery, Liquidating Over $1 Billion as Oil Spikes
{: #israel-iran-strikes-knock-bitcoin-off-its-recovery-liquidating-over-1-billion-as-oil-spikes}

[`cryptobriefing`](https://cryptobriefing.com/israel-iran-airstrikes-crypto-market-impact/) [`cryptobriefing`](https://cryptobriefing.com/oil-prices-surge-iran-israel-bitcoin-drops/) [`cryptobriefing`](https://cryptobriefing.com/iran-missiles-israel-bitcoin-drops-2/) [`cryptobriefing`](https://cryptobriefing.com/iran-airspace-closure-bitcoin-drops/) [`coindesk`](https://www.coindesk.com/tech/2026/06/08/major-cryptocurrencies-under-pressure-as-oil-jumps-3)
{: .sources}

<div class="fng-chip" aria-label="Crypto Fear &amp; Greed Index" title="Crypto Fear &amp; Greed Index. Scale 0 (extreme fear) to 100 (extreme greed)."><span class="fng-label">Fear &amp; Greed</span><span class="fng-value">10<span class="fng-scale">/100</span></span><span class="fng-class">Extreme Fear</span> <span class="fng-delta down">−13 / 7d</span></div>

**What happened.** Israel launched retaliatory airstrikes on Iran overnight, triggering Iranian missile fire in response and a swift closure of airspace around Tehran. Oil jumped roughly 3% on the escalation, and Bitcoin, which had clawed back to $63,700 on Sunday, slid back below $63,000. Crypto markets absorbed over $1 billion in liquidations as the risk-off move spread across asset classes.

**Why it matters.** Bitcoin was already fragile after last week's $1.7 billion in spot ETF outflows, the largest weekly bleed since February 2025, and the geopolitical shock arrived before any macro stabilisation had taken hold. The Fear and Greed Index sits at 10, deep in Extreme Fear and down 13 points over seven days, so there is very little cushion of retail optimism for prices to lean on. A sustained Middle East escalation removes the quiet macro backdrop that a recovery would need.

**The catch.** Analysts at 10xResearch had already pegged last week's slide to sticky US inflation rather than Strategy-specific selling, and the ECB rate decision later this week adds another potential shock. South Korea's KOSPI triggered circuit breakers after an 8%-plus crash, and Saudi Arabia simultaneously slashed Asian crude prices, signalling divergent impulses in energy markets that make the oil spike harder to read cleanly.
