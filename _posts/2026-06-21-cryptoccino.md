## Jaredfromsubway.eth, Ethereum's most notorious MEV predator, loses $7.5M to a counter-exploit.
{: #jaredfromsubwayeth-ethereums-most-notorious-mev-predator-loses-75m-to-a-counter-exploit}

[`cointelegraph_defi`](https://cointelegraph.com/news/notorious-sandwich-attack-bot-jaredfromsubwayeth-exploited-for-75m) [`cryptobriefing`](https://cryptobriefing.com/jaredfromsubway-mev-bot-exploited-15m/)
{: .sources}

<div class="fng-chip" aria-label="Crypto Fear &amp; Greed Index" title="Crypto Fear &amp; Greed Index. Scale 0 (extreme fear) to 100 (extreme greed)."><span class="fng-label">Fear &amp; Greed</span><span class="fng-value">23<span class="fng-scale">/100</span></span><span class="fng-class">Extreme Fear</span> <span class="fng-delta up">+5 / 7d</span></div>

**What happened.** The wallet behind jaredfromsubway.eth, responsible for roughly 70% of sandwich attacks on Ethereum between late 2024 and late 2025, was itself exploited for between $7.5M and $15M depending on the source. An attacker turned the bot's own mechanics against it, draining the operation that had extracted hundreds of millions from ordinary users over its lifetime.

**Why it matters.** This is the most prominent MEV operator in Ethereum's recent history taking a material loss, which signals that sophisticated counter-MEV tactics are maturing. The incident also underscores that running large, exposed on-chain capital pools without robust defensive code is a liability regardless of how sophisticated your offensive strategy is.

**The catch.** The loss figures remain contested: Cointelegraph puts it at $7.5M, Crypto Briefing at over $15M, and no post-mortem from the operator has surfaced. Until someone publishes an on-chain breakdown, operators should treat the higher figure as unverified. The Fear &amp; Greed Index sitting at 23 (Extreme Fear) suggests the market was already fragile before this landed, which limits any broader contagion read from price action alone.
