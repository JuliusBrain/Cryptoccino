## Crypto sheds $390 billion in its worst weekly wipeout since the FTX collapse.

[`coindesk`](https://www.coindesk.com/markets/2026/06/06/bitcoin-ether-eye-worst-weekly-rout-since-ftx-collapse-as-cryptos-shed-usd390-billion) [`decrypt`](https://decrypt.co/370235/bitcoin-dumped-gains-since-trump-reelected-then-some) [`cryptobriefing`](https://cryptobriefing.com/bitcoin-worst-decade-ai-stocks-rotation/) [`coindesk`](https://www.coindesk.com/markets/2026/06/06/bitcoin-back-above-usd61-000-after-rout-leads-to-usd1-6-billion-liquidations)
{: .sources}

<div class="fng-chip" aria-label="Crypto Fear &amp; Greed Index" title="Crypto Fear &amp; Greed Index. Scale 0 (extreme fear) to 100 (extreme greed)."><span class="fng-label">Fear &amp; Greed</span><span class="fng-value">12<span class="fng-scale">/100</span></span><span class="fng-class">Extreme Fear</span> <span class="fng-delta down">−16 / 7d</span></div>

**What happened.** Bitcoin briefly broke below $61,000, triggering $1.6 billion in liquidations before a partial recovery. The broader market shed roughly $390 billion on the week, the steepest drawdown since November 2022. Bitcoin has now erased all post-election gains and is trading more than 50% below its cycle peak.

**Why it matters.** The proximate catalyst is a rotation out of risk assets following stronger-than-expected US jobs data (172,000 in May, unemployment steady at 4.3%), which pushed rate-cut expectations further out. Chip stocks cratered simultaneously, taking crypto with them in a broad risk-off flush. The Fear &amp; Greed Index sits at 12, deep in Extreme Fear and 16 points lower than a week ago, yet the bounce off $61k suggests forced selling may have cleared some of the leverage.

**The catch.** CoinShares logged $5.8 billion in outflows from digital asset products this week, pointing to sustained institutional exit rather than a simple retail panic. The macro setup, strong jobs, no imminent rate cuts, dollar strength from Chinese banks lifting deposit rates, offers no near-term relief catalyst. A second leg down remains the path of least resistance until that changes.
