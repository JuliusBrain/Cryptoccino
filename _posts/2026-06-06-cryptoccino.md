## Zcash patches a vulnerability that allowed undetectable coin counterfeiting, triggering an emergency hard fork and a governance crisis.

[`cryptobriefing`](https://cryptobriefing.com/zcash-governance-concerns-emergency-hard-fork/) [`decrypt`](https://decrypt.co/370184/zcash-crash-wiped-billions-market-cap-can-zec-recover) [`decrypt`](https://decrypt.co/370223/zcash-bug-crisis-shows-privacy-cuts-both-ways-experts-say) [`theblock`](https://www.theblock.co/post/403839/zcash-treasury-cypherpunk-remains-committed-accumulating-5-zec-supply)
{: .sources}

**What happened.** A critical flaw in Zcash's shielded transaction protocol was disclosed and patched via an emergency hard fork. The bug allowed an attacker to mint ZEC undetectably, exploiting the very privacy guarantees that define the network. ZEC's price cratered on the news, wiping billions from its market cap, and Cypherpunk Technologies, a listed Zcash treasury company, saw its shares fall more than 40%.

**Why it matters.** The incident exposes a fundamental tension in privacy coin design: the same cryptographic opacity that shields users also shields any inflation or counterfeiting from on-chain verification. Experts are flagging that the attack class is not unique to Zcash, and that banks and other institutions building on zero-knowledge infrastructure face analogous audit blind spots. The governance fallout compounds the damage, with questions about how long the flaw was known and who approved the hard fork.

**The catch.** Cypherpunk is publicly insisting it will press ahead with accumulating 5% of ZEC supply, calling the sell-off FUD, but its credibility depends heavily on whether the governance process around the patch holds up to scrutiny. The emergency decision-making structure that pushed through the fix is now drawing its own criticism as overly centralised for a project whose identity is built on trustless privacy.
