## Monero Rockets 33% as $120 Million Threads Through an Onchain Laundering Maze, Tether Freezes $72 Million.
{: #monero-rockets-33-as-120-million-threads-through-an-onchain-laundering-maze-tether-freezes-72-million}

[`coindesk`](https://www.coindesk.com/markets/2026/06/12/monero-spiked-to-usd430-as-usd120-million-moved-through-it-then-tether-froze-usd72-million)
{: .sources}

<div class="fng-chip" aria-label="Crypto Fear &amp; Greed Index" title="Crypto Fear &amp; Greed Index. Scale 0 (extreme fear) to 100 (extreme greed)."><span class="fng-label">Fear &amp; Greed</span><span class="fng-value">13<span class="fng-scale">/100</span></span><span class="fng-class">Extreme Fear</span> <span class="fng-delta up">+1 / 7d</span></div>

**What happened.** Roughly $120 million moved through a convoluted onchain route that passed through Monero, sending XMR up 33% to around $438. Tether subsequently froze $72 million linked to the activity, one of the larger reactive freezes the stablecoin issuer has executed.

**Why it matters.** The episode is a live demonstration of the privacy-coin arbitrage that large bad actors still rely on: convert to XMR mid-chain, then exit into stablecoins before anyone can react. That Tether moved as fast as it did narrows the exit window, but $48 million still got through the gap. With the Fear &amp; Greed Index sitting at a rock-bottom 13, a price spike of this size in any asset is worth interrogating for wash or manipulation signals alongside the laundering angle.

**The catch.** Monero's price surge will renew regulator pressure on exchanges that list XMR, several of which have already delisted it in major markets. The freeze also raises a recurring tension: Tether's ability to act unilaterally as a de facto compliance layer is powerful but entirely centralised, and it only covers the USDT leg of the chain.

> **Polish president vetoes MiCA implementation bill for the third time.** President Karol Nawrocki rejected the legislation with only weeks left before the EU's MiCA transitional period closes, leaving Polish crypto firms in a regulatory limbo. Without the bill, local operators face uncertainty over whether they can continue to serve the market under any compliant framework before the deadline. > **SEC's plan to scrap Rule 611 could clear a major path for tokenised stock trading.** Galaxy's Alex Thorn argued that removing the order-protection rule would eliminate a structural barrier that has prevented tokenised equities from being traded on decentralised venues. The proposal is still at the planning stage, but it is the most concrete regulatory signal yet that the SEC is open to secondary-market DeFi activity in equities. [`cointelegraph_regulation`](https://cointelegraph.com/news/sec-plan-to-scrap-rule-611-a-boon-for-tokenized-us-stocks-galaxy?utm_source=rss_feed&utm_medium=rss_tag_regulation&utm_campaign=rss_partner_inbound)

> **Y Combinator throws its weight behind the CLARITY Act.** The accelerator, a backer of Coinbase, Stripe, Kalshi, and OpenAI among others, said it expects crypto to feature in every one of its portfolio companies if the act passes. The public endorsement adds mainstream Silicon Valley cover to what has largely been a crypto-native lobbying effort.
