## Strategy's Bitcoin Premium Collapses as H1 Closes With a $1.3 Trillion Rout
{: #strategys-bitcoin-premium-collapses-as-h1-closes-with-a-13-trillion-rout}

[`theblock`](https://www.theblock.co/post/406438/strategy-loses-bitcoin-premium-enterprise-mnav-dips-below-1) [`bloomberg_crypto`](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-06-26/bitcoin-s-biggest-corporate-buyer-reels-as-funding-edge-vanishes) [`coindesk`](https://www.coindesk.com/markets/2026/06/26/too-big-to-fail-strategy-s-usd13-billion-bitcoin-paper-loss-alone-dwarfs-hundreds-of-prominent-tokens) [`decrypt`](https://decrypt.co/372189/strategy-saylor-volatility-test-strc-new-low-bitcoin-weakness)
{: .sources}

<div class="fng-chip" aria-label="Crypto Fear &amp; Greed Index" title="Crypto Fear &amp; Greed Index. **What happened.** Strategy's enterprise mNAV slipped below 1 for the first time, meaning the market now values the company at less than the bitcoin on its balance sheet. Its STRC preferred shares hit a record low of $71.40, roughly 25% below par, and the firm is sitting on a paper loss of around $13 billion. Bitcoin itself touched $58,000 before a partial bounce to near $60,000, closing out the worst first half for crypto in several years.

**Why it matters.** The mNAV break is structural, not cosmetic. Strategy's entire arbitrage relied on equity premiums and cheap convertible debt to fund perpetual bitcoin accumulation; with both levers impaired, the flywheel stalls. Spot ETFs logged a sixth consecutive day of outflows, shedding roughly $692 million in a single session, the largest single-day exit since May. The Fear &amp; Greed Index sits at 15, deep in extreme fear territory and down 8 points over seven days, yet veteran bottom-hunters are publicly saying conditions are not yet ripe for a sustained reversal.

**The catch.** Ripple CEO Brad Garlinghouse used the moment to argue that Saylor's model has actively damaged broader market sentiment, framing it as financial engineering without utility. The June 30 ex-dividend date and monthly STRC rate reset are now a live watching point: if the preferred dividend resets at a lower rate, it could accelerate further selling pressure in an already thin end-of-quarter tape.
